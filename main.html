About the Project & Curator

Curatorial Statement

When you first arrive in Canada, most of what you are given is practical. Visas, housing, health cards, where to take the bus, how to find work. That information is necessary. But it does not tell you much about where you actually are.

Canada gets described as a multicultural mosaic, and in some ways that is true. You can build a life here without ever leaving your own community. That is one of the real freedoms of this place. But it can also mean that certain questions never come up. Whose country is this, underneath all of it. Which histories get told and which ones quietly disappear.

I am a newcomer too, still in the middle of that process. This festival came out of something simple: I wanted there to be a place where newcomers could encounter this country through its cinema, and start asking some of those questions together. Film opens things up in ways that a guidebook cannot. The hope is that it gives newcomers something to think with.

Yanjun Shen

Yanjun Shen is a graduate student in Migration and Diaspora Studies at Carleton University. She has covered the Toronto International Film Festival for several years and her critical writing engages with Canadian cinema, identity, and migration. She is the creator and host of Film Unspoken, a bilingual film podcast. Her work consistently returns to questions of history, identity, and belonging, with a particular focus on newcomer and immigrant audiences. This festival is one part of that ongoing conversation.
