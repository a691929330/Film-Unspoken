About the Curator

YANJUN SHEN

Yanjun Shen is currently a graduate student in Migration and Diaspora Studies at Carleton University. She is particularly interested in Canadian cinema and films that engage with identity, migration, and politics. She has covered the Toronto International Film Festival for several years, bringing a sustained engagement with film culture and criticism to her curatorial work.

She is also the creator and host of Film Unspoken, a bilingual film podcast that reflects her interest in cinema across languages and cultural contexts. Through both curation and podcasting, she uses film as a medium to bring conversations about history, identity, and belonging to wider audiences, especially newcomers and immigrants.

CURATORIAL STATEMENT

As a newcomer, you may feel the same way I do. When we first arrive in Canada, the information we receive is mostly practical: visas, housing, health cards, transit, and job searching. These things matter, and they help us survive. But they do not tell us much about the country itself, its history, its contradictions, its silences.

Canada is often described as a multicultural mosaic. Many newcomers, myself included, find that it is possible to live well here without ever leaving the comfort of one's own community. That is one of the freedoms of this place. But it can also mean that certain questions never get asked: Whose country is this, really? Which histories have been amplified, and which have been quietly set aside? Beyond multiculturalism, what deeper tensions remain?

I am a newcomer too. While learning about film, I have also been learning about Canada's national identity and history. This festival grew out of that process — out of a desire to create an entry point for newcomers to understand this country through cinema, and in doing so, to better understand where we ourselves stand within it.
